Design rationale
What each node represents
6 outer nodes = 6 integration categories (chat, docs, code, email, calendar, browser). Each gets its own brand color, so users visually recognize "their stack" in the logo itself.
The center hub
Deep purple core with a lighter inner circle. The two-layer circle suggests depth — like a lens or a pupil. It's the "eye of the network," always watching and connecting.
Why 6 beams, not 4 or 8
6 creates a honeycomb/snowflake geometry — perfectly balanced, feels organic rather than mechanical. 4 looks like a compass (directional). 8 gets busy at small sizes.
Animation principle
Beams travel inward (not outward) — data flowing into Prism, not broadcasting out. This reinforces "your knowledge, coming home." Each beam has a slightly different speed for organic feel.
Typeface pairing
Set "Prism" in Inter or Geist at weight 500, tracking -0.03em. Avoid heavy weights — the mark is already bold enough. The name should feel like a companion, not compete.
When to use which color mode
Full color: marketing, splash screens, onboarding. Mono purple: UI headers, nav bars. White knockout: colored backgrounds, swag. Grayscale: legal docs, print, accessibility-critical contexts.
